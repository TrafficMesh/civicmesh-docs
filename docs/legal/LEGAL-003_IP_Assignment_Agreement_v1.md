| Executive Summary
All IP created before incorporation — architecture documents, code, design documents, business plans, training materials, the DC-REG-001 master registry, and all related works — must be formally assigned to the corporation for the corporation to own it. Without this assignment, IP belongs to the founder personally, creating problems for investor due diligence, SR&ED claims (which require corporate ownership of R&D results), and future licensing. This assignment must be executed and dated BEFORE R&D commences. Backdated assignments are not recognized by CRA for SR&ED purposes. |

# 2. Future IP Assignment
All IP created after incorporation that relates to D-Central Group's business is automatically and immediately assigned to D-Central Group Inc. upon creation. Includes:
- All code written for any D-Central platform or product;
- All inventions, discoveries, or innovations related to the D-Central business;
- All training materials and curriculum created for the Training Institute;
- All business development materials, pitch decks, financial models, and strategic documents;
- All improvements to any IP listed in Section 1.
Carve-out: IP created entirely outside working hours, using no company resources, and wholly unrelated to the D-Central business is not subject to future assignment.
