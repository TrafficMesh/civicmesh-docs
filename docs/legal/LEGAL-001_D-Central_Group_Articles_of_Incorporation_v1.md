| Executive Summary
D-Central Group Inc. is the federal holding company for the entire D-Central ecosystem. Incorporated under the Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA) as a Canadian Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC). CCPC status is a non-negotiable prerequisite for refundable SR&ED tax credits, NRC IRAP grants, Mitacs programmes, and ISC awards. The holding company owns all intellectual property across the ecosystem, executes inter-company licensing to operating subsidiaries, and serves as the contracting entity for investor agreements. It does not conduct direct operations — those are performed by operating subsidiaries. |

# 1. CCPC Qualification Requirements


| Requirement | Status | Consequence of Breach |
|---|---|---|
| Incorporated in Canada | Required — CBCA federal incorporation satisfies | Loss of CCPC status — all SR&ED credits become non-refundable |
| Not a public corporation | No public share listing at any time | Loss of refundable SR&ED credits |
| Not controlled by non-residents | 50%+ voting shares held by Canadian residents at all times | Loss of CCPC + SR&ED refundability — material financial impact |
| Not controlled by public corporations | No public company holds 50%+ of voting shares | Loss of CCPC status |




| Action: Before issuing any shares to non-Canadian investors, obtain a legal opinion confirming CCPC status is preserved. Loss of CCPC status converts SR&ED credits from refundable to non-refundable — a direct cash flow impact in the hundreds of thousands. |
