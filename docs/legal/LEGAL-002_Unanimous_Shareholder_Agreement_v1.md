| Executive Summary
A Unanimous Shareholder Agreement (USA) is a contract among all shareholders that restricts, in whole or in part, the powers of the directors to manage the business and affairs of the corporation (CBCA s.146). For a founder-led startup, the USA is the foundational governance document covering founder vesting, board composition, reserved matters, transfer restrictions, anti-dilution, and mission lock provisions that protect the cooperative and open source commitments from investor pressure. |

# 4. Transfer Restrictions


| Right of First Refusal | Selling shareholder must offer shares to existing shareholders pro-rata at proposed price. 30-day exercise window. |
|---|---|
| Board Approval | No transfer without board approval. Board withholds approval if CCPC status at risk. |
| Drag-Along | 75%+ approving a sale drags remaining shareholders on same terms. |
| Tag-Along | If founder sells 10%+ of their shares, minority shareholders may participate pro-rata on same terms. |
| Lock-Up | Founder shares subject to 12-month lock-up from any equity financing round. |


# 5. Mission Lock Provisions
The following cannot be changed without 75% shareholder approval — these provisions survive any change of control:
- Open source licensing of CivicMesh platform core (AGPL v3) cannot be revoked or changed to proprietary — requires 75% approval + 12-month public notice period;
- Cooperative structure of CCSC, CTC, MSSP Cooperative, and Guard Cooperative cannot be dissolved or converted;
- Privacy architecture principles (no national private database, hash-before-store ALPR, on-device audio processing, community data ownership) cannot be abandoned;
- D-Credit cannot be listed on a speculative exchange or structured as a security without 75% approval and securities law compliance;
- Any acquirer must agree to be bound by these mission lock provisions as a condition of shareholder approval of any acquisition.
