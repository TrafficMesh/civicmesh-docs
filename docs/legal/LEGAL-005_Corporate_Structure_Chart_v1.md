# 3. Transfer Pricing Note
As the entity structure grows, intercompany transactions (IP licences, management fees, data fees) are subject to Canada's transfer pricing rules (Income Tax Act s.247). All intercompany transactions must be priced at arm's-length rates. Transfer pricing documentation must be prepared annually. Engage tax counsel with transfer pricing expertise before the first institutional investment round creates valuation benchmarks.
# 4. Key Tax Considerations


| Small Business Deduction | All CCPCs eligible for 9% federal rate on active business income up to $500,000 (vs. 15% general rate). Preserves cash flow in early growth. |
|---|---|
| SR&ED Credits | Refundable SR&ED credits accrue to the CCPC that conducts the R&D and owns (or has sublicence rights to exploit) the results. IP licences must be in place before R&D commences in any subsidiary. |
| Cooperative Patronage | Patronage payments from cooperatives to members are deductible by the cooperative and income to members. Must be structured as patronage dividends under the Income Tax Act. |
| D-Credit Classification | D-Credit must be structured to avoid classification as a security (OSC) or as property income taxable on issuance. Tax and securities counsel required before D-Credit launches. |
