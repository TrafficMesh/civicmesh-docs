| Executive Summary
Ontario's Arthur Wishart Act requires franchisors to provide a disclosure document to prospective franchisees at least 14 days before signing any franchise agreement or receiving any payment. The CivicMesh MSSP Partner Agreement (LEGAL-CM-010) may constitute a franchise agreement under the Act — this depends on whether the definition of 'franchise' (s.1) applies to the MSSP model. Legal counsel must confirm applicability before the first external MSSP Partner Agreement is executed. This document specifies the content required if the Act applies. |
|---|


# 1. Arthur Wishart Act Applicability Analysis


| Element | Definition | CivicMesh MSSP — Does It Apply? |
|---|---|---|
| Grant of right to engage in business of offering/selling goods or services | MSSP deploys CivicMesh platform as a service to municipalities and communities | Likely yes — MSSP offers enforcement platform services |
| Significant degree of control over franchisee's business | CivicMesh Inc. controls: platform standards, SLA requirements, certification requirements, data governance rules, branding requirements | Likely yes — significant operational control |
| Franchise fee or other payment | MSSP pays platform fees, per-node fees, per-citation fees to CivicMesh Inc. | Likely yes — ongoing payment for franchise rights |
| Use of franchisor's trademark | MSSP uses CivicMesh marks in client marketing per brand guidelines | Yes |




| Action: Engage Ontario franchise law counsel to determine whether the Arthur Wishart Act applies to the CivicMesh MSSP model before executing any external MSSP Partner Agreement. If it applies, a full FDD must be prepared and delivered 14 days before execution. The content requirements are specified in Section 2 below. |
