# 1. Ontario Cooperative Corporations Act — Key Requirements


| Requirement | Ontario CCA Section | D-Central Cooperative Obligations | Compliance Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual General Meeting | s.78 — within 6 months of fiscal year end | All D-Central cooperatives (CCSC, CTC, MSSP Coop, Guard Coop) must hold AGM within 6 months of December 31 fiscal year end — by June 30 annually. | AGM calendar set in cooperative bylaws. Automated reminder 90 days before deadline in cooperative governance module. |
| Financial statements | s.79 — presented at AGM | Audited or reviewed financial statements presented to members at AGM. | Annual financial statements prepared by accountant. Audit required for cooperatives with revenue above threshold. |
| Member register | s.44 — maintain register of members | Each cooperative maintains a current member register with name, address (for legal purposes — CivicMesh uses DID as identifier), date of membership. | CCSC membership managed via CivicMesh Member App — DID as primary identifier, legal name collected for T4A purposes only. |
| Director qualifications | s.67 — Canadian resident majority | Majority of directors must be Canadian residents. | All current D-Central cooperative directors are Canadian residents. Board composition reviewed at each election. |
| Dissolution restrictions | s.120 — surplus to members | On dissolution, surplus distributed to members or donated to charitable purpose — not retained by corporate entity. | Cooperative bylaws specify dissolution procedure consistent with s.120. |


# 2. CRA Cooperative Tax Treatment


| Tax Consideration | CRA Position | D-Central Application |
|---|---|---|
| Patronage dividends | Deductible by the cooperative when paid to members. Income to members in the year received. | CCSC patronage distributions are deductible by CCSC and income to members. T4A issued for patronage >$500 CAD. |
| T4A reporting | Cooperatives must issue T4A slips to members receiving patronage dividends > $500 in a calendar year. | Member App tracks cumulative patronage. T4A generated and distributed by January 31 for prior year. |
| D-Credit classification | CRA has not issued specific guidance on cooperative digital currencies. D-Credit should be structured to be received as D-Credit and not taxable until converted to CAD. | Legal opinion on D-Credit tax treatment required before public launch. Structured as a cooperative patronage instrument denominated in cooperative currency units. |
| Non-share capital cooperative | CCSC and most D-Central cooperatives are non-share capital (no shares — member equity through contributions and patronage). Different from business cooperatives with share capital. | Non-share capital structure confirmed in each cooperative's articles. No share issuance possible. |


# 3. DECU — Credit Union Regulatory Path


| Governing legislation | Ontario Credit Unions and Caisses Populaires Act, 2020 (CUCPA 2020). Regulated by Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario (FSRA). |
|---|---|
| Minimum requirements to incorporate | (1) Minimum 150 founding members in Ontario; (2) Minimum $250,000 in member shares; (3) Business plan approved by FSRA; (4) Board of directors meeting FSRA fitness requirements; (5) Insurance through Deposit Insurance Corporation of Ontario (DICO). |
| Realistic timeline | 12–24 months from initial application to FSRA approval and operating licence. This is a Year 3–5 initiative. |
| D-Credit integration path | D-Credit is designed for integration with DECU once DECU is operational. D-Credit balances held in DECU accounts — providing deposit insurance protection and CRA clarity on the tax treatment of D-Credit as a cooperative account balance rather than a digital currency. |
| Interim structure | While DECU is in the regulatory path, D-Credit is managed by CivicMesh Inc. as a cooperative accounting instrument. Patronage is converted to D-Credit at par with CAD. D-Credit can be redeemed for CAD through the platform. This structure avoids securities classification. |
