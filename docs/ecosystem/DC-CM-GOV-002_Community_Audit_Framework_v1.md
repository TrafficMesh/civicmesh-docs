# 1. Annual Audit Structure


| Audit Scope | (1) Data flows: confirm all data flows match documented architecture; (2) Federation events: verify all upward federation events were authorized, logged, and notified; (3) Retention compliance: confirm data deleted within configured retention periods; (4) Consent compliance: verify all data access has valid consent records; (5) AI model performance: review false positive and dismissal rates; (6) Privacy complaints: review resolution of all privacy complaints received |
|---|---|
| Auditor Selection | Independent auditor selected by CCSC board from a pre-approved list. D-Central is not involved in auditor selection. D-Central may submit a proposal if invited but cannot be the sole approved auditor. |
| Access Rights | Auditor has read-only access to: federation event log (full detail), consent records, retention TTL configuration and execution logs, privacy complaint register, AI model performance metrics. No access to: evidence package content, personal data of individual members. |
| Timing | Annual, within 6 months of fiscal year end. Report completed 30 days before CCSC AGM for member review. |


# 2. Community Right to Audit


| Individual Member Rights | Any CCSC member may request their personal audit record: all data held about them, all federation events involving their data, all consent records. Fulfilled within 30 days. |
|---|---|
| Community Collective Rights | CCSC board may commission an extraordinary audit at any time if 20%+ of members petition for one. D-Central must cooperate with extraordinary audit access within 10 business days. |
| Results Publication | Annual audit results (summary findings, pass/fail by category, any corrective actions) published in annual transparency report and on civicmesh.ca/audits. Full report available to CCSC members on request. |
