| Executive Summary
ISC provides grants to Canadian businesses to develop innovative solutions to challenges posted by federal government departments. Phase 1 is up to $150,000 to build a proof of concept over 6 months. Upon successful Phase 1 completion, Phase 2 is up to $1 million for a 2-year full platform build. This document is an application framework — it cannot be submitted until a relevant challenge is posted on the ISC challenge board. The framework documents the CivicMesh value proposition for the departments most likely to post matching challenges. Monitor buyandsell.gc.ca and canada.ca/innovative-solutions weekly. |

# 4. ISC and IRAP Interaction


| Can Both Be Used? | Yes — ISC Phase 1 and IRAP can be applied to the same project, subject to government assistance accounting rules. ISC grant must be reported as government assistance and reduces SR&ED eligible expenditures. |
|---|---|
| Optimal Stack | IRAP covers 80% of eligible salaries monthly. ISC Phase 1 covers project expenses (materials, contractor costs, overhead) as a grant. SR&ED refunds a portion of the remaining eligible costs at year end. All three are complementary. |
| ISC Award Timing | ISC Phase 1 awards are typically disbursed in tranches: 50% on contract signing, 50% on PoC delivery and acceptance. Build this into the cash flow model (FIN-CM-001). |
