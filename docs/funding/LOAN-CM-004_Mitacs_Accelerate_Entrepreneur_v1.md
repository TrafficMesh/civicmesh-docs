| Executive Summary
Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur funds student and postdoctoral entrepreneurs to achieve commercialization goals and further develop the research or technology at the core of their startup, through funded internships at approved incubators. As a current Algonquin College student building a technology startup based on genuine R&D (CivicMesh), Toussaint Redji Jean Baptiste is the exact target profile for this programme. The stipend of up to $7,500 per unit directly offsets founder living costs during the early development phase, complementing IRAP salary reimbursements. |

# 2. Proposed Internship Work Plan
The Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur internship work plan should describe the specific commercialization activities to be funded — not general R&D, but activities that advance the startup toward market:


| Activity | Description | Commercialization Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| OC Transpo pilot proposal development | Research OC Transpo procurement requirements, develop formal pilot proposal document (MKT-TM-GOV-007), prepare officer review portal demo for OC Transpo presentation | Submitted OC Transpo pilot proposal with supporting technical documentation |
| Regulatory compliance research | Commission and review HTA compliance legal opinion (REG-TM-001). Identify regulatory pathway for each CivicMesh module in Ontario. | Legal opinion confirming HTA compliance — removes primary go-to-market risk |
| First CommunityShield HOA client acquisition | Identify, pitch, and sign first 2–3 Ottawa HOA clients using MKT-CS-COM-001 pitch materials | Signed HOA participation agreements (LEGAL-CM-012) generating first revenue |
| IRAP and SR&ED documentation system setup | Set up GitHub organization, Linear workspace with SR&ED tags, Technical Decision Log, time tracking system per SRED-CM-001 | Documentation system operational — enables IRAP claims and SR&ED refunds |
| University research partnership outreach | Approach 3 faculty members at Carleton and uOttawa with Mitacs Accelerate proposals (LOAN-CM-003). Achieve at least 1 committed partnership. | Mitacs Accelerate research unit approved with first university partner |
