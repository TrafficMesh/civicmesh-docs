| Executive Summary
Mitacs Accelerate is the most capital-efficient research programme available to D-Central. For $7,500, you receive 4–6 months of Masters, PhD, or postdoctoral research focused on CivicMesh's hardest technical problems. The research generates publications (credibility), solves genuine technical uncertainties (SR&ED eligible outcomes), and creates relationships with faculty who become academic advocates for the platform. Three simultaneous Mitacs units = $22,500 investment → $67,500 in research value. This is the Mitacs force multiplier described in IRAP-CM-001. |

# 2. What Makes a Faculty Member Say Yes
Understanding the faculty perspective is essential to successful Mitacs partner recruitment. Faculty members need:


| Faculty Need | How CivicMesh Satisfies It |
|---|---|
| A genuine research question with publication potential | Each of the five research questions above is novel, has a clear hypothesis, and targets a top-tier venue. The problems are not solvable with existing published approaches — which is why they are SR&ED eligible. |
| Industry data and access | CivicMesh provides: Ottawa road footage datasets, access to the test node platform, real violation detection outputs for evaluation, and co-authorship on the resulting paper. |
| Student training value | Students gain experience with: production edge AI systems, embedded hardware constraints, open-source development practices, and a real commercial deployment environment. This is stronger CV material than a purely academic project. |
| No IP conflict | CivicMesh is open source. All research outputs are published openly. There is no IP conflict between the academic publication imperative and the commercial interest — open source publications are additive to CivicMesh's credibility, not competitive. |
| Simple relationship | Mitacs handles the paperwork, the payment structure, and the administrative interface. The faculty member focuses on research. D-Central focuses on the commercial application of the results. |


# 3. Mitacs Application Process
- Identify faculty partner at target institution — approach professors working on autonomous vehicles, traffic safety, computer vision, privacy law, or distributed systems;
- Have initial conversation: describe CivicMesh, propose the research question, confirm genuine research interest and publication potential;
- Identify a suitable graduate student (Masters or PhD candidate) who will conduct the research — the student is the primary Mitacs intern;
- Identify an approved incubator (Invest Ottawa, Algonquin Innovation Hub, or Carleton — required for business partner eligibility);
- Complete Mitacs online application: company profile, research project description, faculty supervisor details, student profile, budget;
- Mitacs reviews and approves (typically 4–6 weeks). On approval, company pays $7,500 and Mitacs releases $15,000 to the university;
- Research internship begins — 4–6 months of focused research on CivicMesh's technical problem.
