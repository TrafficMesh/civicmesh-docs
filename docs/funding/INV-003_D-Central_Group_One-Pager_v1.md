# 1. One-Pager Content


| Section | Content |

| Headline | D-Central Group — Community-owned civic safety infrastructure. The cooperative alternative to Flock Safety, Ring, and ShotSpotter. |
| The Problem | Ontario banned automated speed cameras in November 2025. Ottawa lost $38.7M in annual enforcement revenue. 444 Ontario municipalities have the same problem. Existing solutions (Flock Safety, Ring) have no community benefit and face organized resistance. |
| The Solution | CivicMesh: a distributed community safety platform built on existing city vehicles and resident dashcams. Mobile nodes. Human review before every citation. Revenue shared with the community. Open source and auditable. Canadian data residency. |
| Traction | OC Transpo pilot proposal in process. 5 HOA deployments confirmed. IRAP funded. SR&ED documentation active. Training revenue from Day 1. |
| The Government Funding | $300,000–$600,000 in non-dilutive government funding covering 70–85% of development costs before investor capital is required (IRAP, SR&ED, ISC, Mitacs). |
| Market | $7.5B Flock Safety (fixed LPR). $500M+ Nexar (digital twin). $300M ShotSpotter (acoustic). $1.3B Waze (navigation). CivicMesh competes in all four categories with a community-owned, privacy-preserving model that incumbents cannot structurally replicate. |
