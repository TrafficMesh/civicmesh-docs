# 1. Curriculum Modules (8 hours, $1,200 per staff member)


| Module | Content | Duration | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Module A — Platform Architecture | CivicMesh architecture end-to-end: node to evidence package to officer portal to citation. Multi-tenant model. DID/VC trust hierarchy. Where the MSSP fits in the chain. | 90 min | Written exam (25 questions, 75% pass) |
| Module B — NOC Operations | NOC Console operation. Fleet health monitoring. Evidence pipeline metrics. Alert escalation matrix. Priority levels and SLA requirements. Daily checklist execution. | 90 min | Scenario assessment — simulated NOC alert sequence |
| Module C — Federation Request Handling | Federation protocol. Legal authority types. Mandatory approval procedure. Audit log requirements. Community notification obligations. What constitutes a legal review trigger. | 90 min | Case study — 3 federation request scenarios (approve/deny/escalate) |
| Module D — Evidence Chain Integrity | Chain of custody model. What a COC failure means. Investigation triggers. How to document chain failures. Legal implications for the MSSP of chain integrity breaches. | 60 min | Written exam (15 questions) |
| Module E — Privacy Compliance | PIPEDA in the MSSP context. Breach notification obligations. Retention policy enforcement. Audit log integrity. Annual transparency report generation procedure. | 60 min | Written exam (15 questions) |
| Module F — Partner Support | Client onboarding procedures. Community manager training delivery. Officer portal setup and officer training. Escalation pathways for client issues. | 30 min | Not separately assessed — covered in final case study |
| Final Case Study | 3-hour integrated case study: student manages a simulated MSSP client through a major incident — tamper event + concurrent federation request + evidence pipeline outage. All three must be handled concurrently per SOPs. | 3 hours | Pass/fail case study — minimum 70% on evaluation rubric |
