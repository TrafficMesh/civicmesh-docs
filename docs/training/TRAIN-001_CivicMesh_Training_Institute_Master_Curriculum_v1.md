# 1. Institute Mission and Structure


| Executive Summary
The CivicMesh Training Institute is the certification authority for all personnel operating within the CivicMesh ecosystem. It operates as the D-Central Training Cooperative (CTC) — a multi-stakeholder cooperative where D-Central holds the permanent standards anchor seat and accredited providers (Algonquin College, Carleton University, private training companies) are member providers. The Institute generates real revenue from Day 1 — before any enforcement node is deployed — through certification fees paid by security integrators, IT companies, and property managers who want to be ready to deploy CivicMesh when the Ottawa pilot launches. |

# 3. Competency Framework


| Competency Domain | Description | Assessed In |
|---|---|---|
| Hardware Knowledge | Understanding of node hardware components, installation requirements, safety procedures, weatherproofing, power systems, and troubleshooting procedures. | Track 1 practical assessment |
| Platform Architecture | Understanding of CivicMesh data flow, evidence pipeline, federation protocol, DID/VC credentialing, and privacy architecture at the level appropriate to the role. | Tracks 1–5 written exam |
| Privacy and Legal Compliance | Understanding of PIPEDA obligations, consent requirements, federation limitations, data subject rights, and the specific regulatory framework for the certified role. | Tracks 2–5 written exam + case study |
| Operational Procedures | Ability to execute role-specific operational procedures correctly — installation, NOC management, incident response, evidence review — per documented SOPs. | All tracks — scenario assessments |
| Evidence Chain Integrity | Understanding of the CivicMesh chain of custody model, the role of DID/VC credentials in evidence admissibility, and the consequences of chain integrity failures. | Tracks 1, 2, 5 — case study |
| Cooperative Governance | Understanding of the cooperative structure, member rights and obligations, transparency reporting, and the CivicMesh community ownership model. | Track 3 — community manager specific |


# 4. Quality Standards for Accredited Providers


| Standard | Requirement | Verification Method |
|---|---|---|
| Curriculum fidelity | Provider delivers curriculum that meets CTC competency standards without material omission or modification. Additions are permitted — reductions are not. | Annual curriculum audit by CTC Standards Committee |
| Instructor qualification | Technical track instructors (Tracks 1, 2) must hold the certification level they are teaching + 1 (i.e., L1 instructor must hold L2 minimum). Non-technical tracks: subject matter expertise in the relevant domain. | Instructor credential verification at provider accreditation |
| Assessment integrity | Practical assessments must be proctored (in-person or approved remote proctoring). Written exams administered through approved LMS with plagiarism detection. | Random assessment audit — 5% of assessments reviewed by CTC |
| Credential issuance | Credentials issued only to candidates who have passed all assessments. No credential issued on incomplete assessment. | Technical: CTC trust registry shows issuing provider DID — anomalies investigated |
| Record retention | Assessment records retained for 5 years. Available to CTC for audit on 10 business days notice. | Annual audit request |

# 6. Day-1 Revenue Strategy


| Why training generates revenue before nodes are deployed | Security integrators, IT companies, and property management companies in Ottawa will pay to certify their staff NOW in anticipation of CivicMesh deployments. Being pre-certified means they can win contracts faster when the pilot launches. |
|---|---|
| Target Month-1 cohort | 50 L1 certifications at $299 = $14,950 gross revenue. 60% to D-Central (founding provider) = $8,970. CTC retains $5,980. This is real cash flow before a single OC Transpo contract is signed. |
| Algonquin partnership path | Approach Algonquin College entrepreneurship office with a proposal to offer CivicMesh Node Technician as a Continuing Education certificate. Algonquin's continuing education division has existing relationships with Ottawa security companies. Their distribution channel is worth more than the 60% revenue share they receive. |
| Online-first delivery | All Track 1 L1 theory content is online — no classroom required. This means zero incremental cost to deliver the first 100 certifications. Practical assessments require a test vehicle — use the founder's own vehicle for the first cohort. |
