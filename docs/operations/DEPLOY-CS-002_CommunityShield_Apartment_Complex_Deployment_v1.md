# 1. What Landlords Can and Cannot Deploy


| Location | Permitted? | Restrictions | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Building entrances (exterior) | Yes | Must post notice that cameras are in use. | Landlord property — standard security camera authority |
| Parking lots and exterior common areas | Yes | Must post notice. | Landlord property |
| Lobby, mail room, elevator lobbies | Yes | Must post notice. No audio recording. | Landlord property |
| Hallways inside the building | Yes, with restrictions | Cameras must not be positioned to capture tenants' unit entrances in a way that enables tracking of when tenants are home/away. | Residential Tenancies Act privacy provisions — REG-CS-001 |
| Inside tenant units | No | Tenants have exclusive possession of their units. Landlord cameras inside units are illegal. | Residential Tenancies Act s.29 |
| Tenant-controlled doorbell cameras (tenant opt-in) | Yes — tenant's own device | Tenant connects their own device voluntarily. Landlord has no access to tenant's device footage. | Tenant property — tenant controls |


# 2. Tenant Opt-In and Rent Credit Programme


| What it is | Tenants who connect their own doorbell camera or dashcam to the community CommunityShield network receive a monthly rent credit funded by their node's patronage earnings. |
|---|---|
| How it works | Tenant installs compatible doorbell camera. Tenant creates CivicMesh Member App account. Tenant opts in to community network. Landlord's Manager Dashboard shows tenant's enrollment (anonymized — landlord sees 'Unit 4B — 1 active node' not the tenant's identity). CCSC patronage flows to tenant's Member App account. Tenant redeems as rent credit by generating a credit voucher in Member App and submitting to landlord. |
| Landlord benefit | Higher tenant satisfaction and retention. Insurance premium reduction certification (coverage density). Fine revenue from parking and access violations on landlord property. |
| Tenant benefit | $20–$60/month estimated rent credit at average Ottawa violation density. Insurance premium reduction on renter's insurance. Community safety improvement in their building. |
| Cannot be required | Tenant opt-in is voluntary. The Residential Tenancies Act prohibits landlords from requiring tenants to install surveillance equipment as a condition of tenancy. This programme must be structured as a voluntary benefit, not a requirement. |
