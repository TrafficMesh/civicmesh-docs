# 1. Phase 2 Expansion Logic
The OC Transpo pilot produces the data package needed to open every subsequent conversation. The pitch to the City CAO, Public Works director, and Bylaw chief is not conceptual — it is a presentation of actual Ottawa enforcement revenue, actual violation data by corridor, and an actual proposal to replicate across the broader city fleet.


| Fleet Category | Vehicles (est.) | Node Tier | Violation Types Added | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bylaw enforcement vehicles | 40–60 vehicles | Tier 1 permanent | Accessible parking, noise, snow route violations, expired plates → police queue | Phase 2A — Month 1–3 of expansion |
| Public Works inspection trucks | 20–30 vehicles | Tier 1 permanent | Pothole detection (digital twin), illegal dumping, sign/signal inventory | Phase 2A — Month 1–3 |
| Garbage and recycling trucks | 80–120 vehicles | Tier 1 permanent | Snow route violations (overlapping coverage), illegal dumping, accessible parking | Phase 2B — Month 3–6 |

| Street sweepers (Tier 2 seasonal) | 30–40 sweepers | Tier 2 seasonal (Apr–Oct) | Lane marking condition, spring road survey for digital twin | Phase 2C — Month 6–9 |
| Parks and recreation vehicles | 20–30 vehicles | Tier 1 permanent | Accessible parking at parks, noise in parks, illegal dumping | Phase 2C — Month 6–9 |
| City contractors (Tier 3 kit) | 50–100 contractors active at any time | Tier 3 contractor kit | Construction zone enforcement (auto-activated), digital twin contribution | Phase 2A — contractor kit programme launch |


# 2. City Revenue Model


| Revenue Source | Estimated Annual Ottawa Value | Municipal Share | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bus stop / bus lane enforcement (OC Transpo pilot annualized) | $300K–$600K | 65% | Based on NYC MTA ACE data scaled to Ottawa fleet size |
| Snow route parking violations | $400K–$800K | 65% | High-volume, overnight coverage — currently near-zero enforcement due to staffing |
| Accessible parking violations | $150K–$300K | 65% | Chronically under-enforced — city fleet provides city-wide passive coverage |
| Expired/suspended plate alerts | No direct revenue — police referral | N/A | Value is public safety outcome + police efficiency, not revenue |
| Public Works inspection savings | $500K–$1.5M in avoided manual survey costs | City retains 100% of savings | Conservative estimate based on current survey programme cost |
| Digital twin data marketplace contribution | $50K–$150K annually from insurer and planner subscriptions | 20% city data royalty | Grows as coverage density increases |


# 3. Contractor Kit Programme — City Procurement Integration


| Procurement clause | Add the following to all applicable city contractor agreements: 'Contractor shall receive, install, operate, and return a CivicMesh Contractor Node Kit per LEGAL-CM-015. All data captured by the kit is city property. Tampering with the kit is a material breach of this contract.' |
|---|---|
| Kit inventory management | City Fleet Services manages kit inventory. D-Central Shield provides: kit supply and replenishment, installation guides, quarterly kit inspection and recertification, NOC monitoring for all contractor kits. |
| Contractor benefit | Construction zone protection: Contractor kit presence automatically activates a 40km/h enforcement envelope around the contractor's work site. Drivers approaching active work zones are alerted via MeshNav. This directly protects the contractor's workers. |
| Phase 2A launch | Pilot with 10 road construction contractors. Expand to all applicable contractors in Phase 2B. |


# 4. Political Preparation


| Stakeholder | Concern | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| City Council — fiscal conservatives | Cost of implementation | OC Transpo pilot shows ROI. Phase 2 hardware costs $500K (500 nodes × $1,000 avg). Year 1 enforcement revenue projected $1.2M–$2.1M. Payback period: 3–5 months. |
| City Council — civil liberties | Privacy and surveillance | Open source, auditable, privacy-preserving architecture. Human review before every citation. Public camera map. Annual transparency report. Not a fixed camera — no known location to avoid. |
| Ottawa Police Service | Coordination on expired plate referrals, stolen vehicle alerts | TrafficMesh generates police referrals, not police replacements. Bylaw handles bylaw violations. Police handle criminal matters. We increase their intelligence with ALPR data they currently don't have. |
| CUPE / City Workers | Job impact for bylaw officers | TrafficMesh does not replace bylaw officers — it amplifies them. Officers move from passive camera monitoring to active community enforcement where human judgment is essential. |
