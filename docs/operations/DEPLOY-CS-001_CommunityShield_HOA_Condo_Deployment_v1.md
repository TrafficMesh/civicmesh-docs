# 3. Resident Consent — Mandatory Notice Requirements


| What MUST be communicated to residents before activation | (1) The community is deploying a CivicMesh community safety camera network; (2) Camera locations (map provided); (3) What data is collected — event-triggered clips only, no continuous recording; (4) Who can access the footage — community manager for safety events, law enforcement only via formal request with notification; (5) How residents can opt in to the node owner programme to earn revenue; (6) How to request their data, correct it, or delete it; (7) How to opt out entirely. |
|---|---|
| Notice format | Written letter delivered to all residential units AND posted on community bulletin board AND sent via community email list if available. Template provided in the community manager toolkit. |
| Minimum notice period | 14 days before system activation for non-residential properties. 30 days for residential properties (apartment buildings, condo corporations, HOAs with resident units). |
| Board/Strata approval | Most condo corporations require a board resolution to authorize surveillance systems. Community manager must confirm board approval before installation proceeds. Template board resolution language provided. |


# 4. Fine Revenue Activation — Private Property Enforcement


| What requires activation | By default, CommunityShield captures safety footage but does not process enforcement actions. To activate parking and bylaw enforcement (and associated revenue), the community manager must: (1) confirm the community has authority to issue fines under their bylaws or parking rules, (2) configure violation types in Manager Dashboard, (3) confirm fine amount schedule with CommunityShield Inc. |
|---|---|
| Processing fees | CommunityShield retains 15% of collected fines as platform processing fee. Community retains 60%. Node owner members receive 25% (distributed via CCSC). |
| First enforcement review | Community manager must review and approve the first 10 enforcement incidents manually (Standard Review, not Fast-Track) before Fast-Track automation is enabled. This training period ensures the community manager is comfortable with the review process and the evidence standard. |
