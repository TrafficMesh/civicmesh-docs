# 1. Slide-by-Slide Narrative


| Slide | Title | Key Message | Speaker Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Your Telematics Data Has a Problem | Self-reported telematics data: 40% of drivers modify their behaviour during the telematics period. Your pricing is based on observed performance, not actual risk. | Open with the insurer's pain, not your product. Every actuary in the room knows the self-reported telematics problem. |
| 2 | CivicMesh Is Third-Party Validated Telematics | Evidence-grade driving behaviour data from the same infrastructure that issues enforcement citations. Not self-reported — verified. | 'When we say a driver has clean behaviour, we mean no violations attributed to their vehicle in 12 months of continuous monitoring by city fleet nodes, civilian dashcams, and delivery vehicles covering every road in Ottawa.' |
| 3 | The Data Product — Three Tiers | Tier 1: Aggregate corridor risk for actuarial modelling. Tier 2: Consent-gated individual driving record API for underwriting. Tier 3: Incident evidence for claims and subrogation. | Show the API documentation. Make it concrete — they should be able to imagine how their underwriting team would use Tier 2. |
| 4 | Tier 1: Actuarial Gold | Corridor risk scores. Violation density heatmaps. Road condition index. Every road in Ottawa, continuously updated. | 'Your Ottawa actuarial team currently uses postal code as a proxy for risk. CivicMesh gives you the actual risk — by corridor, by time of day, by season, by weather condition.' |
| 5 | Tier 2: Individual Record at Renewal | With policyholder consent, pull a 12-month verified driving record — lane discipline, following distance, time-of-day profile, clean period confirmation. | 'No more relying on the customer's self-reported history. No more relying on your own dongle that the customer knows is there. Third-party validated data they opted in to share.' |
| 6 | Tier 3: Claims and Subrogation | Incident evidence packages — video, GPS, timestamp, chain of custody — for specific identified claims. | 'How many subrogation cases fail because you can't establish fault? Every CivicMesh-network vehicle is a potential witness.' |
| 7 | The Premium Discount Flywheel | Offer discounts → more customers opt in → more data → better pricing → larger discounts → more customers opt in. | 'Waze for insurance. Your customers opt in because they get cheaper insurance. You benefit because your models get better.' |
| 8 | The Fraud Reduction Opportunity | Staged collisions, false claims, exaggerated claims — all become much harder when the incident is documented by CivicMesh nodes. | Give a dollar figure for fraud costs in Ontario auto insurance. |
| 9 | Competitive Positioning | Insurer who partners with CivicMesh gets: better data, lower fraud, better pricing accuracy, marketing differentiation, community goodwill. | 'First insurer in Ottawa to offer the CivicMesh discount wins the opt-in market.' |
| 10 | Privacy and Compliance | PIPEDA compliant. Consent-gated. No adverse use. Annual audit. All data stays in Canada. | Address the compliance question before legal raises it. Show the PIA summary. |
| 11 | Pricing | Tier 1 annual subscription: $50K–$150K/year for Ottawa data. Tier 2 per-query: $15–$25 at renewal. Tier 3 per-incident: $50–$75. | 'Less than one avoided fraud case pays for a year of Tier 3 access.' |
| 12 | Next Steps | Tier 1 pilot: Ottawa aggregate data for your actuarial team for 90 days. No commitment. See the data before you sign anything. | Make the first ask free. Get the data in front of their actuarial team and let the value speak. |
