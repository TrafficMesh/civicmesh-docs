# 2. Objection Matrix — Universal


| Objection | Source | Answer | Supporting Document |
|---|---|---|---|
| 'It's surveillance' | Civil liberties advocates, opposition councillors | Human review before every citation. Open source. Auditable. Community-owned. Public camera map. Annual transparency report. No continuous recording. | MKT-CM-003 + REG-CM-001 |
| 'Data privacy concerns' | Privacy advocates, provincial privacy commissioner | PIPEDA PIA completed. On-device blurring. Canadian data residency. No commercial data broker connection. Community controls their own data. | REG-CM-001 + DC-CM-PRV-001 |
| 'Vendor lock-in' | Procurement officers, IT departments | Open source — city can self-host, fork, or switch operators. AGPL licence prevents proprietary lock-in. All data in standard formats. | LEGAL-CM-016 + DC-CM-ARCH-001 |
| 'We already have Flock Safety / Ring' | IT departments familiar with incumbents | Flock: US company, private database, no community benefit, DeFlock backlash in 30+ cities. CivicMesh: Canadian, community-owned, cooperative revenue share, no national private database. | MKT-CM-GOV-002 + DC-STRAT-005 |
| 'Too expensive' | Finance departments | No capital infrastructure. Node hardware at cost price for city clients. ROI positive within 3–5 months based on OC Transpo pilot data. | FIN-TM-001 + FIN-CM-002 |
| 'What if the province bans it too?' | Risk-averse procurement officers | Human review requirement is the legal distinction. Ontario's ASE ban targeted automated enforcement — not officer-assisted mobile evidence. Legal opinion on file. | REG-TM-001 |
