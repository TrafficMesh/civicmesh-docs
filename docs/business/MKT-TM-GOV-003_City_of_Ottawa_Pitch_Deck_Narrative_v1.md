# 1. The City Value Proposition Beyond OC Transpo


| Executive Summary
The OC Transpo pitch is about enforcement revenue. The City of Ottawa pitch is broader: TrafficMesh is infrastructure. The same nodes that generate citation revenue also maintain the city's digital twin, automatically identify infrastructure defects, eliminate manual road survey costs, and provide real-time situational awareness to emergency management. The pitch to the CAO is not just 'we replace the ASE cameras' — it is 'we make every city vehicle smarter and every city department more efficient simultaneously.' |
|---|

# 3. The Contractor Kit Argument


| The question | 'How do you get coverage on residential streets without deploying cameras everywhere?' |
|---|---|
| The answer | 'Every city contractor already drives those streets. We add a 5-minute self-install kit to the contractor equipment package. When the contractor is on city property, the city's enforcement network moves with them.' |
| The cost argument | 'You are not buying 500 camera systems. You are buying 50 kits that rotate through 500 vehicles over the course of a year. The capital cost is a fraction of fixed camera infrastructure and the coverage is orders of magnitude better.' |
| The political argument | 'Construction zone protection: when a contractor kit is active, it automatically enforces reduced speed in the work zone. The city's enforcement network protects the contractor's workers.' |


# 4. Key Objections and Answers


| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| 'The Ford government might ban this too' | The ASE ban targeted fixed cameras with automated ticket issuance. TrafficMesh requires human officer review — legally and technically. The ban's stated rationale (removing human judgment from enforcement) does not apply to a system that mandates human review. |
| 'How do we explain this to residents?' | The public consultation toolkit (MKT-CM-003) is designed for exactly this. Camera map. Annual transparency report. Revenue share. 'Your garbage truck helps pay for the pothole on your street.' |
| 'What about procurement rules?' | Unsolicited proposal process or RFP. D-Central is Ottawa-based, open-source, CCPC — checks all local procurement preference boxes. |
| 'Is this a surveillance system?' | No. It is an enforcement evidence system with mandatory human review, open source code, published architecture, and a cooperative ownership model where residents earn from participation. The difference matters legally and politically. |
