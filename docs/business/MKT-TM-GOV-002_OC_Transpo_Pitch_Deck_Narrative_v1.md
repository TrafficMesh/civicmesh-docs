# 1. Slide-by-Slide Narrative


| Slide | Title | Key Message | Speaker Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bus Lane Enforcement Works — Here's the Proof | NYC MTA ACE programme: $225M in 24 months, bus speeds up 5%, collisions down 20%. | Open with the NYC data. Do not open with the product. Open with the outcome they want: faster, safer buses and enforcement revenue. |
| 2 | Ottawa's Bus Lanes Are Under-Enforced | Show bus speed data and collision data on Transitway and Albert/Slater vs. NYC comparables. State enforcement gap. | Use OC Transpo's own operational data if accessible. 'Your drivers deal with this every day.' |
| 3 | The ASE Ban Creates an Opportunity | Fixed cameras are banned. Mobile enforcement on city vehicles is not covered. Ottawa is first to market. | Stress: this is not a workaround — it is a different legal category. REG-TM-001 confirms. Legal opinion available. |
| 4 | What TrafficMesh Is | 30-second system explanation: node on bus → AI detects violation → officer reviews → citation issued. Human review always. | Keep this simple. 'Camera on bus, officer reviews before any ticket is issued.' That's the whole story for this slide. |
| 5 | What It Looks Like — Officer Portal Demo | Screenshot or live demo of officer portal with a real evidence package (from test vehicle on Ottawa routes). | This is the most important slide. Show real Ottawa footage. Real plate read. Real confidence score. Real officer review workflow. Make it tangible. |
| 6 | The NYC Legal Precedent | How NYC MTA ACE works legally. Parallel to OC Transpo authority in Ontario. HTA sections that apply. | 'NYC transit authority had the same question you're having. Here's how they resolved it. Here's the Ontario equivalent.' |
| 7 | The Revenue Model | OC Transpo receives 65% of net citation revenue. Platform fee: 20%. CCSC community fund: 10%. OC Transpo reinvestment fund: 5%. | Be specific about the OC Transpo share. 'Conservatively, based on NYC volumes scaled to Ottawa: $225,000–$900,000 in the first 6-month pilot.' |
| 8 | The Privacy Architecture | Human review. No continuous recording. Open source. City owns the data. Public camera map. Annual transparency report. | Lead privacy before they ask. 'We built this to address every concern you'll hear from councillors and riders before they raise them.' |
| 9 | The Pilot Proposal | 30 buses. 3 corridors. 6 months. Violation types: bus stop obstruction + bus lane. 2 officers for review queue. | Make the ask small and specific. 'We're not asking for a citywide rollout. We're asking for 30 buses on 3 routes for 6 months.' |
| 10 | The Installation Process | Week-by-week installation timeline. Minimal disruption to bus operations. Fleet garage access 02:00–06:00. | Address the operational concern before it's raised. 'Your garage team won't even know we were there.' |
| 11 | KPIs and Reporting | Monthly KPI report: citation volume, revenue, dismissal rate, system uptime, contested citation rate. | 'You'll know exactly what's working and what isn't. Monthly, in writing, with full data.' |
| 12 | Recommended Next Steps | Legal team reviews REG-TM-001 opinion. Technical team demos the officer portal. Procurement initiates pilot agreement process. | Three specific, named next steps. Give them something to do before you leave the room. |
