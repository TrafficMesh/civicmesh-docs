# Talk Structure


| Section | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| The extraction problem | 8 min | Platform economics extracts value from communities. Uber, Airbnb, Ring, Flock — the pattern is identical. Communities provide value; corporations capture it. The political backlash is structural, not accidental. |
| The cooperative alternative | 10 min | D-Central Group as a case study in cooperative technology. Five verticals. DID/VC credentialing as cooperative identity infrastructure. D-Credit as cooperative economic layer. The mission-moat identity. |
| CivicMesh as proof of concept | 15 min | How CivicMesh applies cooperative principles to community safety infrastructure. The revenue share model. The governance rights. The privacy-by-design architecture. Ottawa as the first deployment. |
| The replication challenge | 7 min | Why investor-owned competitors cannot copy this model. The replication paradox. What the cooperative economy requires to scale nationally. |
| Open discussion | 5 min | What does a cooperative technology ecosystem look like at scale? What policy changes would accelerate it? |
