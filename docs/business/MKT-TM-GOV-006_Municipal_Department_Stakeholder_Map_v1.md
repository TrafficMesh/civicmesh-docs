# 1. City of Ottawa Decision-Maker Map


| Department | Target Contact | Their Priority | Budget Line | Entry Point | Procurement Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| OC Transpo | VP Operations or Director of Service Delivery | Bus lane compliance, transit revenue, operational data | OC Transpo operating budget + capital budget | Invest Ottawa introduction or direct outreach after legal opinion | Unsolicited pilot proposal → OC Transpo board approval → procurement |
| Bylaw and Regulatory Services | Chief of Bylaw Services | Enforcement coverage without officer headcount increase | Bylaw operating budget | City CFO introduction after revenue gap brief | RFP or unsolicited proposal for pilot programme |
| Public Works | Director, Infrastructure Management | Road condition data, manual survey cost reduction | Capital maintenance budget + operational survey budget | Public Works innovation office or City CAO referral | Pilot under existing technology procurement or innovative solutions pathway |
| Ottawa Police Service — Traffic Unit | Staff Sergeant, Traffic Section | ALPR coverage expansion, stolen vehicle detection, resource efficiency | OPS operational budget | Formal introduction through City of Ottawa — not cold outreach to OPS | Memorandum of Understanding for data sharing — not a procurement contract |
| Urban Planning | Manager, Transportation Planning | Traffic counts, pedestrian/cyclist data, Vision Zero evidence base | Urban planning study budget | Planning conference presentation or city innovation office referral | Research partnership or data subscription agreement — low procurement complexity |
| City CAO | Chief Administrative Officer | Revenue gap, cost reduction, innovation positioning | Executive office | Mayor's office introduction via city council champion | Executive briefing → CAO-level pilot authorization |
| City CFO | Chief Financial Officer | $38.7M revenue gap, enforcement ROI | Finance department | Revenue gap brief (MKT-TM-GOV-001) delivered via CAO or councillor | CFO signs off on revenue share agreement — not a technology procurement |


# 2. The Champion Strategy


| The ideal champion | An OC Transpo or City of Ottawa employee who: (1) understands the revenue gap problem, (2) has seen the officer review portal demo, (3) wants to be associated with the solution. Their internal advocacy is worth more than any external pitch. |
|---|---|
| How to identify them | Attend Ottawa Transportation Committee meetings. Attend Invest Ottawa events where OC Transpo and city staff present. The person who is asking hard questions about the ASE revenue gap at public meetings is your champion. |
| What the champion needs | Enough information to brief their supervisor in 5 minutes: the revenue gap number, the NYC precedent, the legal opinion status, the pilot scope, and the next step. MKT-TM-GOV-001 (revenue gap brief) is designed for this purpose. |
