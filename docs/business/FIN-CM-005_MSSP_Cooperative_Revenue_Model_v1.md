# 1. MSSP Partner Revenue Model


| Revenue Stream | How It Works | MSSP Margin | Scale Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Citation revenue share | MSSP retains 20% of gross citation revenue from all nodes under management. City receives 65-70%, CCSC 10-15%. | Gross: 20% of citation revenue. Net after NOC costs: 8–12% of gross citation revenue. | Linear with node count and enforcement corridor density. |
| NOC management fees | MSSP charges clients $20–$40/node/month. MSSP pays CivicMesh platform $8–$15/node/month. Margin: $5–$25/node/month. | $5–$25/node/month depending on tier and volume. | Linear with node count. |
| Installation fees | MSSP charges clients $500–$2,500/node installation. MSSP pays technician $150–$500. Margin: $200–$2,000 per installation. | $200–$2,000 per installation (one-time). | Linear with new deployments. Declines as market matures. |
| MSSP Cooperative patronage return | Members receive X% of net platform fee revenue (post-D-Central operating costs) pro-rata by nodes under management. | Additional 5–10% uplift on platform revenue proportional to managed node count. | Scales with total platform revenue — compounds as platform grows. |
| Training certification fees | MSSP Staff Track 2 certification: MSSP pays $1,200/staff member. MSSP can charge this to clients as part of implementation fees. | Pass-through — no direct margin but enables client billing. | Linear with MSSP headcount growth. |

# 3. MSSP Cooperative Patronage Model


| Patronage pool | After D-Central operating costs, [X]% of net platform fee revenue is pooled for cooperative distribution. Distributed to MSSP Cooperative members pro-rata by their nodes under management as a percentage of total managed network. |
|---|---|
| Example at 1,000 total managed nodes, MSSP A has 200 | MSSP A's share: 200/1,000 = 20% of cooperative patronage pool. If pool is $50,000/year: MSSP A receives $10,000 cooperative patronage in addition to their direct revenue. |
| Why this matters for MSSP adoption | The cooperative patronage means a small MSSP with 50 nodes under management benefits from the growth of the entire network — not just their own deployments. This incentivizes all MSSP operators to grow the platform, not just their own client base. |
