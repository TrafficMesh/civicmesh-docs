# 1. Why Flock Safety Is a Political Risk


| Flock Safety Issue | Documented Evidence | Political Risk for Adopting Municipality |
|---|---|---|
| National private ALPR database | Flock operates a national database of plate reads shared across all client communities. Data accessible to law enforcement without individual community consent or notification. | Municipalities face 'secret surveillance' accusations. DeFlock.me has 30+ organized community groups fighting Flock installations. |
| Federal immigration enforcement data sharing | Investigative reporting confirms Flock data has been accessed by ICE and immigration authorities. | Sanctuary city municipalities face direct political contradiction. Legal exposure in jurisdictions with anti-cooperation ordinances. |
| Unpermitted installations | Forbes reported in 2024 that Flock installed hundreds of devices on public roads without required permits. | Procurement liability. If the same practice occurs in your city, council faces questions about oversight. |
| Zero community benefit | Flock extracts data from communities and returns nothing. No revenue share, no community ownership, no cooperative governance. | In a post-ASE-ban environment where the community benefit argument matters, Flock has no answer. |
| Proprietary and unauditable | No one outside Flock can verify what their system does or doesn't do with your community's data. | Open source requirement policies in progressive municipalities explicitly exclude Flock. |


# 2. The CivicMesh Alternative — Five Structural Differences


| Dimension | Flock Safety | CivicMesh |
|---|---|---|
| Data ownership | Flock owns the data. National private database. | Community owns the data. No national private database. Hash-before-store. |
| Community benefit | Zero — no revenue share, no governance rights. | CCSC cooperative revenue share. Member governance rights. Annual transparency report. |
| Open source | Proprietary — unauditable. | AGPL v3 — full source code published. Anyone can audit. |
| Canadian compliance | US company — CLOUD Act exposure for Canadian data. No specific Canadian privacy compliance. | Canadian CCPC. PIPEDA PIA completed. Canadian data residency. No CLOUD Act exposure. |
| Political resilience | 30+ organized community groups opposing Flock nationally. Flock cameras being removed in growing number of US cities. | Community owns the network. Community earns from it. Political constituency for continuation, not removal. |


# 3. The One-Line Pitch for Council


| CivicMesh is what Flock Safety would be if the community owned it, the data stayed in Canada, and the enforcement revenue came back to residents. |
