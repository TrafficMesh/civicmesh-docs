# 1. The Government Funding Advantage


| Executive Summary
Before walking into an investor meeting, D-Central Group will have received approximately $300,000–$600,000 in non-dilutive government funding covering 70–85% of development costs. The investor is not being asked to fund development risk — they are being asked to fund scaling a working, government-validated product. This is a categorically different investment proposition. |

# 5. The Investor Framing


| Wrong framing (avoid) | 'We are building a traffic camera company in Ottawa.' |
|---|---|
| Right framing | 'We are building the protocol layer for community-owned civic safety infrastructure — the platform that Flock Safety, Ring, and ShotSpotter wish they had built, positioned at the intersection of a $38M Ottawa revenue gap, a $7.5B market category, and a cooperative model that makes our users into our most powerful political advocates.' |
| Government validation argument | 'The NRC's Industrial Technology Advisor has reviewed our technical uncertainty and approved IRAP funding. CRA has accepted our SR&ED claim structure. An ISC Phase 1 application is pending with Transport Canada. When the federal government funds your R&D, validates your technical merit, and your first client is a publicly-funded transit authority — you are not a startup anymore. You are a growth-stage company that hasn't needed outside capital yet.' |
| The ask in context | '$500,000 to accelerate what the government has already validated. Not to build something new — to scale something proven.' |
