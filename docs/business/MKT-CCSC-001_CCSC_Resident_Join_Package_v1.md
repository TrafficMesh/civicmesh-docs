# 2. The Plain-Language Value Statement


| What you get by joining CCSC
• A share of enforcement revenue when your dashcam or doorbell camera helps document a road safety violation — typically $30–$100/month for an active Ottawa driver.
• A reduction on your auto insurance premium if you opt in to the verified driving record programme — typically 8–15% annually.
• A vote in how Ottawa's community safety infrastructure is governed — one member, one vote.
• The knowledge that your dashcam is contributing to safer roads, not just sitting in your glove box.
• The right to opt out at any time. No penalty. No minimum commitment. |
|---|


# 3. What CCSC Is NOT


| Misconception | Truth |
|---|---|
| 'It's a surveillance programme' | CivicMesh never stores continuous footage. Your camera only captures short clips when an AI model detects a potential violation. Faces are blurred on your device before any footage leaves it. |
| 'The government is watching you' | CivicMesh is a cooperative — it is community-owned, not government-owned. Law enforcement can only access your footage through a formal legal request, and you are notified within 48 hours if that happens. |
| 'You're reporting on your neighbours' | You are contributing to community safety infrastructure the same way a witness provides a statement. The violation is independently documented — you don't decide who is cited, an officer does. |
| 'It's like Ring/Amazon' | Ring is owned by Amazon. Your footage is stored on Amazon's servers. Your data is used by Amazon. CivicMesh is owned by the cooperative — your data is stored on community-owned infrastructure and is yours. |
