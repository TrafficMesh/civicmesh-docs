# Design Brief


| Tone | Accessible, honest, not corporate. Plain language throughout. Avoid jargon. |
|---|---|
| Lead message | This presentation must lead with privacy protections, not technology features. The public audience wants to know: is this watching me? What happens to my footage? The answer to both must come in the first 3 slides. |
| Camera map visual | Show the actual camera location map for this community. Transparency about where cameras are is more persuasive than any privacy promise. |
| Revenue slide | Make the revenue share concrete for this specific community. 'Based on similar deployments, we expect X violations per month. At 60% HOA share, that is $Y per month to your community.' |
| Q&A preparation | Slides 14–15 should be a printed FAQ handout, not slides. People take the FAQ home and read it. Slides disappear when the presentation ends. |
