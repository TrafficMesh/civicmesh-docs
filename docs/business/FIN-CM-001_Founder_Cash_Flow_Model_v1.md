# 3. Business Credit Card Float Management


| The Float Problem | Expenses are charged to the business credit card when incurred. IRAP reimburses approximately 30–45 days after the monthly milestone report. SR&ED refunds after filing. The business credit card carries a float of roughly 45–60 days of eligible expenses at any time. |
|---|---|
| Float Size Estimate | At $5,000–$8,000/month in eligible expenses (hardware, cloud, contractors), the float is approximately $10,000–$16,000 at any given time. The credit card limit must be at least this amount. |
| Recommended Card | RBC Avion Business or TD Business Travel Visa. Apply at incorporation. Request $20,000–$25,000 limit. Pay balance in full monthly from payroll account after IRAP reimbursements arrive. |
| Cash Buffer | Maintain a minimum $15,000 CAD cash buffer in the business bank account at all times. This covers: payroll tax remittances due on the 15th, credit card balance payment, and unexpected expenses. Do not draw below this buffer. |
| Alert Threshold | If cash balance drops below $20,000, trigger a review: check IRAP milestone report status, check SR&ED refund ETA, check HOA client billing, and consider whether to reduce expenses temporarily. |


# 4. Salary Strategy


| Defensible Salary Range | $70,000–$80,000 per year is defensible for a technical founder with IRAP. At $75,000, IRAP covers $60,000 (80%) and you pay $15,000. After SR&ED on the remaining 20%, net cost is approximately $12,000–$14,000/year. This is the most capital-efficient way to pay yourself. |
|---|---|
| Why Not Higher | $100,000 is possible but requires IRAP to accept it as market-rate for a student founder. $75,000–$80,000 is market-rate for a senior developer in Ottawa without founder markup. Discuss with your ITA before setting the salary. |
| Why Not Lower | Paying yourself less than $60,000 is a false economy. IRAP covers 80% — the incremental cost of an additional $10,000 salary is only $2,000 out of pocket (20%). Take the market rate. |
| Payroll Setup | Must be set up through a formal payroll system (QuickBooks Payroll, Wagepoint, or similar) before the first paycheque. Payroll deductions (CPP, EI, income tax) must be remitted to CRA by the 15th of the following month. This is non-negotiable — late remittances attract penalties. |
| Mitacs Entrepreneur Stipend | If Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur is approved ($7,500/unit), this is paid as a stipend — it is not a salary and does not reduce IRAP-eligible salary. It supplements the salary draw. |
