    FIN-CM-001 Founder Cash Flow Model v1¶

    FIN-CM-001 D-CENTRAL GROUP D-Central Group Inc. Founder Cash Flow Model — 18-Month Pre-Investor Build

    Document ID FIN-CM-001
    Version 1.0
    Status PRIORITY — complete before any spending or salary draw
    Date May 2026
    Author Toussaint Redji Jean Baptiste
    Classification Confidential — Financial
    Purpose Month-by-month cash flow projection for 18-month development period. Governs salary draw, IRAP timing, SR&ED refund planning, credit card float management.
    Related Documents IRAP-CM-001

    | Executive Summary This document is a framework for the founder cash flow model. The actual model should be built in a spreadsheet (FIN-CM-001.xlsx — to be created) with monthly columns for 18 months. The purpose is to ensure the company never runs out of cash during the development phase, to understand the IRAP reimbursement timing, to plan the SR&ED annual refund, and to manage the business credit card float between spending and reimbursement. The framework below provides the structure and key assumptions. | |---|

    Complete the spreadsheet version of this model (FIN-CM-001.xlsx) before drawing any salary, before submitting the IRAP application, and before making any R&D expenditure. The cash flow model is the instrument panel for the entire funding strategy.

    1. Revenue Sources — Monthly Timing¶

    Revenue Source Amount Timing Notes
    IRAP salary reimbursement — founder 80% of eligible monthly salary Monthly — approximately 30–45 days after milestone report submission Depends on ITA approval and monthly report acceptance. First payment typically Month 2–3.
    IRAP salary reimbursement — additional staff 80% of eligible monthly salary per staff member Monthly — same lag as above Add to model when each staff member is hired
    IRAP contractor reimbursement 50% of eligible contractor invoices Monthly — with regular milestone report Contractor invoices must be submitted with milestone report
    SR&ED federal ITC refund Up to 35% of net eligible expenditures (after IRAP reduction) Annual — typically June/July after April filing Budget conservatively: assume 90 days after filing
    Ontario SR&ED top-up Up to 30% of net eligible expenditures Annual — with federal filing Some lag from federal; consolidate in same month estimate
    CommunityShield HOA NOC fees $[Monthly fee per HOA] × number of HOA clients Monthly — from first HOA client signing Model conservatively: 3 HOA clients by Month 6, 5 by Month 9
    Training certification fees $299–$1,200 per candidate × candidates per month Monthly — as candidates enroll Conservative: 5 candidates/month from Month 2, 15/month by Month 6
    Mitacs Accelerate contribution return $7,500 per unit (company contribution required) One-time per unit — company pays out, does not receive This is a NET COST — but generates $15,000 in research value
    ISC Phase 1 — tranche 1 50% of award (up to $75,000) On contract signing — approximately Month 10–12 if applied Month 6 Only if ISC challenge matches and application is accepted
    ISC Phase 1 — tranche 2 50% of award (up to $75,000) On PoC acceptance — approximately Month 16–18

    3. Business Credit Card Float Management¶

    The Float Problem Expenses are charged to the business credit card when incurred. IRAP reimburses approximately 30–45 days after the monthly milestone report. SR&ED refunds after filing. The business credit card carries a float of roughly 45–60 days of eligible expenses at any time.
    Float Size Estimate At $5,000–$8,000/month in eligible expenses (hardware, cloud, contractors), the float is approximately $10,000–$16,000 at any given time. The credit card limit must be at least this amount.
    Recommended Card RBC Avion Business or TD Business Travel Visa. Apply at incorporation. Request $20,000–$25,000 limit. Pay balance in full monthly from payroll account after IRAP reimbursements arrive.
    Cash Buffer Maintain a minimum $15,000 CAD cash buffer in the business bank account at all times. This covers: payroll tax remittances due on the 15th, credit card balance payment, and unexpected expenses. Do not draw below this buffer.
    Alert Threshold If cash balance drops below $20,000, trigger a review: check IRAP milestone report status, check SR&ED refund ETA, check HOA client billing, and consider whether to reduce expenses temporarily.

    4. Salary Strategy¶

    Defensible Salary Range $70,000–$80,000 per year is defensible for a technical founder with IRAP. At $75,000, IRAP covers $60,000 (80%) and you pay $15,000. After SR&ED on the remaining 20%, net cost is approximately $12,000–$14,000/year. This is the most capital-efficient way to pay yourself.
    Why Not Higher $100,000 is possible but requires IRAP to accept it as market-rate for a student founder. $75,000–$80,000 is market-rate for a senior developer in Ottawa without founder markup. Discuss with your ITA before setting the salary.
    Why Not Lower Paying yourself less than $60,000 is a false economy. IRAP covers 80% — the incremental cost of an additional $10,000 salary is only $2,000 out of pocket (20%). Take the market rate.
    Payroll Setup Must be set up through a formal payroll system (QuickBooks Payroll, Wagepoint, or similar) before the first paycheque. Payroll deductions (CPP, EI, income tax) must be remitted to CRA by the 15th of the following month. This is non-negotiable — late remittances attract penalties.
    Mitacs Entrepreneur Stipend If Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur is approved ($7,500/unit), this is paid as a stipend — it is not a salary and does not reduce IRAP-eligible salary. It supplements the salary draw.
