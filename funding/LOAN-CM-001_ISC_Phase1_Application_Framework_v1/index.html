    | Executive Summary ISC provides grants to Canadian businesses to develop innovative solutions to challenges posted by federal government departments. Phase 1 is up to $150,000 to build a proof of concept over 6 months. Upon successful Phase 1 completion, Phase 2 is up to $1 million for a 2-year full platform build. This document is an application framework — it cannot be submitted until a relevant challenge is posted on the ISC challenge board. The framework documents the CivicMesh value proposition for the departments most likely to post matching challenges. Monitor buyandsell.gc.ca and canada.ca/innovative-solutions weekly. | |---|

    2. Phase 1 PoC Scope (for Transport Canada Road Safety Challenge)¶

    Challenge Statement (hypothetical) "Transport Canada seeks innovative solutions for mobile, community-based road safety enforcement that complement existing fixed infrastructure while addressing privacy and community acceptance challenges."
    Proposed Solution CivicMesh TrafficMesh — a privacy-preserving, open-source, community-owned mobile enforcement platform that uses existing city fleet vehicles as enforcement nodes, with human review before every citation and full community revenue sharing.
    Phase 1 Deliverable Working proof of concept demonstrating: (a) edge AI violation detection on a vehicle-mounted node, (b) evidence package generation with cryptographic chain of custody, (c) officer review portal with human sign-off before citation, (d) privacy-preserving data governance with community audit log, (e) performance benchmarks on Ottawa road footage.
    Phase 1 Timeline 6 months from award — aligns with IRAP-TM-001 Milestones M1 through M3
    Ottawa Advantage Ottawa is the seat of federal government. Transport Canada HQ is in Ottawa. An Ottawa PoC with direct Transport Canada visibility maximizes Phase 2 award probability.

    3. Phase 1 Application Narrative Template¶

    The following narrative framework should be customized to the specific challenge language when a matching challenge is posted:

    Section Content Framework
    Problem Statement Ontario's November 2025 automated speed enforcement ban eliminated $38.7M in annual enforcement revenue for Ottawa alone and created a province-wide gap in road safety infrastructure. Existing fixed camera alternatives are: vulnerable to vandalism (known locations), easily avoided (GPS-mapped by civilians), politically vulnerable (perceived as revenue extraction with no community benefit), and unable to cover residential streets, school zones, or construction sites without permanent installation.
    Proposed Innovation CivicMesh TrafficMesh is a distributed mobile enforcement network that converts existing city fleet vehicles (buses, plows, sweepers, bylaw vehicles) into enforcement nodes. The innovation is in five areas: (1) edge AI violation detection optimized for Canadian road conditions; (2) privacy-preserving evidence architecture with on-device blurring and cryptographic chain of custody; (3) community-owned cooperative model with revenue sharing to participants; (4) W3C DID/VC credentialing for every node, technician, and MSSP operator; (5) federated data governance allowing community-level audit of every law enforcement data access.
    Technical Approach [Describe the Phase 1 technical work plan — 6-month milestone schedule from IRAP-CM-001 Section 4. Reference the five technical uncertainty areas. Describe the documentation system that will generate contemporaneous evidence of systematic investigation.]
    Commercial Potential Target market: all 444 municipalities in Ontario for fleet-mounted enforcement ($X estimated annual revenue potential). Secondary: 3,500+ HOAs and condo corporations in Ottawa for CommunityShield deployments. National expansion to all provinces following Ontario pilot. International (US, UK, Australia) in Years 4–6.
    Team Toussaint Redji Jean Baptiste — founder and lead developer. Currently at Algonquin College. Mitacs research partnerships with [Carleton / uOttawa] for [AI / legal] research. [Additional team members at time of application].
    Why ISC Phase 1 (not Phase 2) CivicMesh is at TRL 3–4 at time of application — concept validated, prototype in early development. Phase 1 will advance to TRL 6 (technology demonstrated in relevant environment — Ottawa pilot). Phase 2 application will follow with demonstrated OC Transpo pilot results.

    4. ISC and IRAP Interaction¶

    Can Both Be Used? Yes — ISC Phase 1 and IRAP can be applied to the same project, subject to government assistance accounting rules. ISC grant must be reported as government assistance and reduces SR&ED eligible expenditures.
    Optimal Stack IRAP covers 80% of eligible salaries monthly. ISC Phase 1 covers project expenses (materials, contractor costs, overhead) as a grant. SR&ED refunds a portion of the remaining eligible costs at year end. All three are complementary.
    ISC Award Timing ISC Phase 1 awards are typically disbursed in tranches: 50% on contract signing, 50% on PoC delivery and acceptance. Build this into the cash flow model (FIN-CM-001).
